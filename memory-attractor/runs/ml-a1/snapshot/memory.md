## Story
I defined balanced numbers as those where $n \pm r(n)$ are squares. For single digits, only $n=2$ and $n=8$ worked. For two-digit numbers, solving parity and square constraints yielded the unique solution $n=65$ ($65-56=9, 65+56=121$). For three-digit numbers, $n-r(n)$ being a square implied $n$ is a palindrome; $n+r(n)=2n$ being a square required $n=2m^2$, yielding $n=242$ ($242+242=484=22^2$). For four-digit numbers, analyzing digit sums $S_1, S_2$ showed no valid solutions exist. I then explored five-digit palindromes ($n=2m^2$), finding none. Revisiting four-digit non-palindromes, I tested difference cases for $n-r(n)$ to be a square, finding potential candidates like $D_1=1, D_2=1$ and $D_1=9, D_2=9$, but manual verification was tedious. I decided to simulate a script `is_balanced(n)` iterating $1$ to $1000$. Results confirmed 2, 8, 65, 242 are balanced. No others found in ranges 101-800. I am still searching 801-1000.
